Block-level elements form a visible block on a page. They surround paragraphs or groupings of content. A block level element appears on a new line following the content that precedes it. Any content that follows a block level element also appears on a new line. Block-level elements are usually structural elements on the page.

Block-level elements represent headings, paragraphs, lists, navigation menues, footers.

Inline elements are contained within block-line elements and surround small parts of the content.An inline element will not cause a new line appear in the document.
